Course Overview

This 4-Level professional course equips electrical engineering students with both theoretical ground and practical application in substation design and protection systems. It covers everything from control philosophy to advanced protection schemes, calculation techniques, and field interfacing practices, providing industry-aligned, job-ready competencies.

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

  • Understand the structure and control of conventional and automated substations.
  • Interpret and design substation SLDs, schematics, and signal lists.
  • Perform key sizing calculations for CTs, VTs, cables, batteries, and relays.
  • Configure relay settings and develop safe interlocking schemes.
  • Prepare and review submittals including design documents and vendor drawings.

Course Structure

Level 1: Foundations of Substation Design

Session 1: Welcome to the Grid

Substation types, voltage levels, layouts, and system roles.

Session 2: Brains of the System

Conventional vs SAS Substation Control

Hierarchy and components in conventional and automated systems.

Session 3: Protection System Basics

Philosophies, protection elements, attributes, relay types.

Session 4: Relay Types and Applications

Overcurrent, differential, and distance relays.

Level 2: Advanced Substation Design & Drawings [Base Design]

Session 1: Single Line Diagrams (SLD)

Main SLD purpose, structure, IEC conventions.

Session 2: Relay & Metering SLD

CT/VT integration, relay layout.

Session 3: Distance Protection Schemes

Pilot-aided and non-pilot-aided schemes, zone settings.

Session 4: Interlocking Schemes, Alarm list

Logic, maloperation prevention.

Session 5: AC/DC SLDs

AC/DC SLDs

Level 3: Engineering Calculations & Design Sizing

Session 1: Breaker and Cable Sizing

IEC-based sizing for AC/DC breakers and cables.

Session 2: Auxiliary Transformer & Zig-Zag Sizing

Roles, calculation steps.

Session 3: Battery and Charger Sizing

DC load profile, battery sizing.

Session 4: CT and VT Fundamentals

CT classes, sizing, adequacy; VT basics.

Session 5: Relay Setting Calculations

Overcurrent, distance, unit protection.

Level 4: Practical Interfacing & Field Applications

Session 1: Wiring Intelligence

Session 2: Wiring Intelligence (Cont'd)

Control Schematics Session with symbols and wiring notations.

Reading Protection Schematics and how to trace reactions.

Exploring Key Circuits used in Electrical Control and Protection.

Exercise: Trace circuit logic in schematic

Session 3: The Last Meter

Cable Schedules & Termination Charts

Assignment: Fill termination chart from drawing

Session 4: Panel Contents

Local/remote/protection schematics, signal list creation.

Assignment: Signal list + protection schematic.

Session 5: Final Wrap-Up and Submittals

Document requirements, vendor deviations, Telecom Networks, starting real projects.

About the Instructor

Ahmed Metwally is a substation engineering manager with over 16 years of hands-on experience in AIS and GIS substations, with a focus on control and protection schemes. Throughout his career, he has worked closely with multidisciplinary teams across primary, secondary, and telecom systems, gaining practical insight into the challenges of real-world project execution.

He has contributed to cost-saving initiatives that helped optimize designs and vendor specifications, leading to measurable improvements in efficiency. His academic background includes studies in Japanese Corporate Management through Harvard Business School and ongoing MBA studies, reflecting a continuous commitment to learning and development.

Ahmed believes in sharing practical knowledge in a structured, accessible way, and is passionate about helping engineering students bridge the gap between theory and application.
